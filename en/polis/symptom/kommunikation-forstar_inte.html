Communication – the person doesn’t understand

The person with dementia is frustrated by not understanding what people around her say, and not being able to express herself.

What can I do:

    Try to prevent the person from feeling stupid. When you talk, keep this in mind:
  • Adapt the conversation.
  • Sit down, make eye contact, talk slowly.
  • Use a soft body language.
  • Wait for a reply before you keep talking.
  • Let the conversation take time.
  • Instead of asking questions, try to give clues that could lead to an answer.
  • Rather ask if the person is hungry than what he has eaten.

Why does the person act this way:

The self esteem gets affected for someone who doesn’t understand a question. It gets hard to express something important. Often this frustration results in agitation.
