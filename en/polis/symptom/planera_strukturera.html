Planning and structurizing

The person cannot take in more than one instruction.

What can I do:

Think about the following:

  • Think about your body language.
  • Never show that you are stressed.
  • Give information and instructions in a positive way.
  • Speak slowly in a confident and calm way.
  • Speak in a low voice.
  • Make several pauses.
  • Use short sentences and give simple explanations.
  • Don’t ask the person to make a choice between different options.
  • Ask one question at a time and allow for time to reply. She might need time to sort out her thoughts.
  • Be hands-on:
    • Ask for example: ”Do you want to go home?” instead of saying: ”Your family is worried about you.”
    • Use you body language to emphasise what you are saying. You can for example point to show a car or a phone.

Why does the person act this way:

For a person with dementia it becomes increasingly difficult to think, plan, and carry out activities. It also gets increasingly difficult to understand and do more than one thing in an activity.
