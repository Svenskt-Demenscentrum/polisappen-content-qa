Restlessness and picking at things

The person paces up and down. Perhaps pulls a door handle or repeatedly picks at things.

What can I do:

Unless the person seems to suffer, there is no reason to interrupt this behaviour. If the situation becomes difficult, you can distract the person like this:

  • Talk about the clothes: ”What a nice hat”, ”That jacket seems warm”, etc.
  • Point at for example a bird, a child that plays, or something else that can stop the behaviour.
  • If you feel comfortable and it suits the situation, hum a song or play some music on your cell phone.
  • If possible, offer something to eat or drink in order to gain energi and rest a while.
  • Some become calm from touching, others don’t.

Why does the person act this way:

People with dementia might lose the ability to halt and stop this kind of behaviour. It might also be a way to handle anxiety and to get rid of stored-up energy.
