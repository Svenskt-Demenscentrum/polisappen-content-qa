Memory problems – living in the past

The old woman is on her way to her mother or to work.

What can you do:

Even if it may seem strange, for this person her world is as real as yours. When you meet an old person who says she is going to her parents, it’s no use trying to explain or argue. It’s better to just confirm that feeling.

You can for example say: ”I understand that you are worried and want to go home.” Ask if you may help her. Ask questions about her parents. This is not being dishonest, only confirming the feeling and distracting her thoughts into another direction. This usually makes it easier to move forward and help the person.

Why does the person act this way:

People with dementia may experience that they live in a different time and age, and in another place. Really old people, for example, might see themselves as very young, and therefore being on their way to their parents’ home or to work.
