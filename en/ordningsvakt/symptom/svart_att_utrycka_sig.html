Difficulties to understand and express oneself

It’s hard to express oneself, understand what others are saying, finding words or form sentences. Words might be used incorrectly or not make any sense in a certain context.

What can I do:

Try to convey a feeling of safety and calm. Help them understand as much as possible what’s going on.

A light touch, like putting your hand on the person’s shoulder or offering holding your arm, can sometimes have a smoothing effect.

Try to distract a person who seems to be stuck in a thought or a situation by trying to get her to think about something else.

Confirm and play along if you think it feels right. The important thing is to reach the person so that she can get the correct assistance.

Why does the person act this way:

Because of the dementia the person has problems expressing herself and understanding spoken language.
