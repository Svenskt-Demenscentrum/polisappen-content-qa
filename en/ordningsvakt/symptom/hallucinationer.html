Hallucinations

The person sees things and talks to people that don’t exist.

What can I do:

  • It’s important that you try to imagine the situation the person is in. You can do that by sharing the experience or confirming the feeling.
  • You can say: ”I can see that you’re scared, how can I help?”
  • If the person is aware that she is hallucinating you can say: ”I understand that you are seeing something, but I can’t see it.”
  • If she sees for example unpleasant animals and thinks they are real, the best thing to do is to listen and confirm. You can say: ”I understand that the animals seem scary to you. Come on, let’s leave.”

Why does the person act this way:

Hallucinations are the pictures in your mind which the damaged brain evokes. This can appear as the disease has progressed a while. Some people get scared and anxious, while others aren’t bothered that much.
